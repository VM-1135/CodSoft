Indian Army

The Indian Army is the land-based branch and largest component of the Indian Armed Forces. The President of India is the Supreme Commander of the Indian Army,[3] and its professional head is the Chief of Army Staff (COAS). The Indian Army was established on 1 April 1895 alongside the long established presidency armies of the East India Company, which too were absorbed into it in 1903.The primary mission of the Indian Army is to ensure national security and national unity, to defend the nation from external aggression and internal threats, and to maintain peace and security within its borders. It conducts humanitarian rescue operations during natural calamities and other disturbances, such as Operation Surya Hope, and can also be requisitioned by the government to cope with internal threats. It is a major component of national power, alongside the Indian Navy and the Indian Air Force.[7] The army has been involved in four wars with neighbouring Pakistan, one with China and one with Afghanistan

Conflicts and Wars

Since gaining independence, the Indian military has fought in four wars: three against Pakistan and one against China. The Indian military has not faced defeat since 1962

  • 1947-48 Kashmir War
  • 1962 India-China War
  • 1965 India-Pakistan War
  • 1971 India-Pakistan War
  • 1984 Siachen Conflict
  • 1999 Kargil Conflict

The Indian Army has contributed to UN peacekeeping missions in many ways, including:

  • Providing troops: India has contributed over 253,000 personnel to 49 of the 72 UN peacekeeping missions. India is one of the largest contributors of troops to UN peacekeeping missions, with around 5,900 troops currently deployed in 12 UN missions.
  • Providing force commanders: India has provided and continues to provide force commanders for UN missions.
  • Providing military observers: India has contributed impartial military observers.
  • Providing staff officers: India has contributed committed staff officers.
